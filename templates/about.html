Why We Exist

We believe in freedom through technology. And we're convinced that everyone, not just a select few, should have access to powerful hacking and automation tools. Look at countries like Russia, Ukraine, or China. They've built entire ecosystems for ethical hackers and tinkerers, complete with their own microcontroller brands Arduino, M5Stack, and more. But here in India? We're still largely dependent on imported devices, overpriced gadgets, or tools simply not designed with Indian users in mind. When we saw devices like the Flipper Zero listed for ₹10,000–₹40,000 on Indian marketplaces, it hit us: most Indian students, tech enthusiasts, and ethical hackers just can't afford these tools.

So we asked ourselves: Why not us?

Why can't we build this hardware for our own people? That question sparked the beginning of HiveFusion Lab. We're driven by the belief that we can empower a new generation of ethical hackers, makers, and engineers with affordable, usable, and Indian-built hardware.

Our story

It all started in 2024.

As a cybersecurity enthusiast, I (Amir) loved exploring hacking hardware. But even building a basic deauther device felt unnecessarily complex for a beginner. I had to rely on NodeMCU boards, which were bulky, had pins sticking out, and were never meant for pocket use or transport.

I wanted something clean, compact, and purpose-built. At the same time, Sunil, an electronics nerd like me, also wanted to break out of the usual college projects and make something real. Though we weren’t close friends initially, we kept crossing paths hacking Wi-Fi in labs, messing around with reverse shells, pulling pranks on phones learning, laughing, and realizing: we think the same. There were no flipper-like devices available here. No Indian company making this hardware. Getting hands on international gear was a pain.

Thats when we said:
Lets stop waiting. Lets build our own.
And so, HiveFusion Lab was born.
